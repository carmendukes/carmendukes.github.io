Glare

perhaps all the ones and zeros have gone to my head—
replaced the delight in ordinary things like sweet
summer blueberries and my bare feet on dune grass

this body feels like it was built for beautiful
the small: a speckled hummingbird egg
the big: a star-filled sky above a moonstoned beach

I bite my nails thinking possibility without
a beginning or end, and when I remind myself
containers are only safe if one remembers to close the lid

I've retyped this entry twenty times
Over-indexing on the right words instead
of the sensation of unknowing. I get torn
between chasing sunlight and chasing perfect

like me, the machine prefers perfection
from an imperfect being those squiggly red
lines under imperfectly perfect thoughts

lately, I've been noticing how the sun, when low
scatters light around coast oaks and sage scrub
flocks of pelicans sparkle as they bounce on ocean swells
suddenly every being is a silhouette outlined in gold


and this screen

bright and blindingly white

disappears right before my eyes
