Glare

perhaps all the ones and zeros have gone to my head—replaced the delight in ordinary things like sweet summer blueberries and my bare feet on dune grass.

this body feels like it was built for beautiful things. the small: a speckled hummingbird egg. the big: a star-filled sky above a moonstoned beach.

I bite my nails thinking possibility without a beginning, end, or scale and containers are only safe if one remembers to close the lid.

I've typed this entry twenty times. Over-indexing on the right words instead of the sensation of unknowing. I get torn between chasing sunlight and chasing perfection.

funny how the machine likes perfection from an imperfect being (me). those squiggly red lines under the wrong word, wrong grammar, wrong thought.

lately, I've been noticing how the sun scatters light around coast oaks and sage scrub. flocks of brown pelicans sparkle and bob on ocean swells. every being now a silhouette outlined in gold.


and this screen

bright and blindingly white

disappears right before my eyes
